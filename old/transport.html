Transport

Public Transport

Tram

The tram network is the easiest way to move around the city. Several lines connect the center with the venue area, and departures are frequent throughout the day. A single ticket usually covers the full journey and makes tournament mornings pleasantly low-stress.

Bus

Buses are a useful backup or complement to the tram system, especially if you stay outside the central core. They are generally reliable, easy to combine with other public transport, and often the simplest way to reach hotels, parking areas, and the venue from nearby districts.

Getting to Gothenburg

By Plane

Gothenburg is well connected by air, and the airport offers several convenient onward travel options into the city. Once you arrive, airport buses, rental cars, and local transport make it straightforward to continue toward your hotel or the venue.

By Train

Train travel is often the smoothest option if you are arriving from elsewhere in Sweden or nearby countries. The central station is well placed, with strong public transport links onward to the tournament area and most accommodation options.

By Car

Arriving by car gives flexibility, especially if you are bringing equipment or travelling as a group. Road access into Gothenburg is generally straightforward, though it is a good idea to check parking plans in advance and allow a little extra time on busy tournament mornings.
